Hunger

The Problem

The problem we are trying to solve is malnourishment. Hunger means not having enough healthy food to grow, learn, and live well. When kids miss meals, they struggle in school. Adults without enough food can’t work or care for their families. Without good food, people stay stuck in poverty and poor health.

  • Over 820 million people worldwide are chronically undernourished each year.
  • Nearly 150 million children under five experience stunted growth due to malnutrition.
  • Malnourishment contributes to nearly half of all child deaths globally.
  • Food insecurity affects one in nine people on our planet.

Facts About Food Hunger

  • 1 in 11 people don’t have enough food.
  • Climate change, conflict, and money problems make hunger worse.
  • Every 10 seconds, a child dies from hunger.
  • Over 733 million people go hungry.
  • Most hungry people are women and children.
  • In the U.S., over 35 million people struggled to get enough food in 2023.
  • About one-third of all food is wasted worldwide.
  • Hunger increases the risk of diseases like diabetes and heart problems.
  • Hungry kids are more likely to struggle in school.
  • Hunger costs the world over $1 trillion each year.

The Solution

We will start "The Top Refrigerator" to help people get healthy food. We will set up community fridges stocked with extra food from stores and farms, and use vans to deliver meals to neighborhoods in need. We’ll also teach simple cooking and nutrition classes so families can make healthy meals on a budget.

What We Will Do

  • Set up community fridges and food hubs with healthy food from local partners.
  • Work with farmers, stores, and restaurants to collect extra food.
  • Use mobile vans to deliver fresh food to neighborhoods in need.
  • Teach simple cooking and nutrition classes.
  • Organize volunteers to help distribute food.

Ways You Can Help

Every bit helps. Donate, volunteer, or share our mission to fight hunger. Your support makes a real difference in our community.

  • Donate non-perishable food items, fresh produce, or funds to keep our fridges and mobile pantries stocked.
  • Volunteer your time sorting and distributing meals at community sites or in our mobile vans.
  • Create fundraising campaigns—online or in your neighborhood—to support operational costs.
  • Advocate for policies that address the root causes of hunger, from food waste reform to nutrition assistance programs.
  • Share our story on social media to raise awareness and inspire others to join the fight against hunger.
